From 'Why Are There Still So Many Jobs?': The history of automation shows a paradox - as machines replace human labor, new jobs emerge. Why? Because automation increases productivity, lowers costs, creates new industries. But AI is different - it automates cognitive tasks, not just manual ones. The question isn't whether jobs will exist, but what skills they'll require. Adaptability, creativity, human connection - these remain uniquely human.
