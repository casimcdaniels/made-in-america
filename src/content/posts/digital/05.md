Frey & Osborne's 2017 study found that 47% of US jobs are at high risk of computerization. As they wrote: "It's not about job elimination, it's about job transformation. Jobs requiring creativity, social intelligence, and complex problem-solving are less susceptible." But here's what gets lost in the headlines: the real challenge is ensuring workers can transition. We need massive retraining programs, not panic. History shows us that technological disruption creates as many opportunities as it destroys‚Äîbut only if we prepare.
