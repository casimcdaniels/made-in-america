Frey & Osborne's 2017 study found that 47% of US jobs are at high risk of computerization. But here's what gets lost in the headlines: it's not about job elimination, it's about job transformation. Their research shows that jobs requiring creativity, social intelligence, and complex problem-solving are less susceptible. The real challenge? Ensuring workers can transition. We need massive retraining programs, not panic. History shows us that technological disruption creates as many opportunities as it destroys - but only if we prepare.
