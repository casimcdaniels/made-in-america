Agrarian Era (1600s-1820):  

    Crèvecœur, J. Hector St. John - Letters from an American Farmer (1782) — Observations on colonial farming and American identity 

    Adams, John & Abigail - Personal letters (1770s–1780s) — Farm life, household labor, and colonial work 

Market Revolution Era (1820-1880):  

    Lowell Mill Girls - Letters and writings (1830s–1840s) - Factory work, strikes, and organizing 

    Robinson, Harriet Hanson - Loom and Spindle (1898) - Memoir of Lowell mill work and labor organizing 

    Douglass, Frederick - Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass (1845) - Slave labor, skilled trades, and free vs. slave labor 

    Northup, Solomon - Twelve Years a Slave (1853) - Free Black man kidnapped into slavery; skilled labor under slavery 

    de Tocqueville, Alexis - Democracy in America (1835) - Observations on manufacturing and class relations 

    Heighton, William - The Mechanics' Free Press (1820s) — Artisan organizing and early labor newspapers 

Industrial Era (1880–1970):  

    Sinclair, Upton - The Jungle (1906) - Meatpacking conditions in Chicago 

    1906 U.S. House Report on Chicago Stockyards - Congressional investigation of meatpacking conditions 

    Oral histories of Texas oil technicians - Worker experiences with automation and industrial machinery 

Post-Industrial Era (1970–2000):  

    NASA "Hidden Figures" - Accounts of Katherine Johnson, Dorothy Vaughan, Mary Jackson — Women in computing and mathematics 

    Hopper, Grace - Computer scientist and Rear Admiral - Early programming and women in computing 

    Borg, Anita - Computer scientist - Women's exclusion from tech as it became more prestigious 

Digital Era (2000–present):  

    Autor, H. David - "Why Are There Still So Many Jobs? The History and Future of Workplace Automation" (2015) — Labor economics and automation 

    Wiener, Norbert - The Human Use of Human Beings (1950) — Cybernetics, automation, and human-machine relationships
